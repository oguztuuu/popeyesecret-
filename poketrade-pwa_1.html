🎓 CARD GRADING GUIDE
Understanding card condition is essential for accurate pricing. Each grade affects value significantly — a card in PSA 10 can be worth 5-10x its raw NM price.
RAW CARD CONDITIONS
NEAR MINT (NM) / MINT
100% VALUE
The gold standard for raw cards. Card appears virtually unplayed with sharp corners, no visible scratches or whitening on edges. Surface is clean with no marks. Slight handling wear may exist under magnification but invisible to the naked eye. This is the baseline for PriceCharting prices.
LIGHTLY PLAYED (LP)
~75-85% VALUE
Minor wear visible on corners (slight rounding), light scratches on surface, very minor edge whitening. The card was played but cared for. Common in collections that were played without sleeves. Still looks good from a distance.
MODERATELY PLAYED (MP)
~50-65% VALUE
Noticeable wear — rounded corners, edge whitening visible at normal viewing distance, light creases possible, surface scratches present. Card was actively played. Still structurally sound with no major damage.
HEAVILY PLAYED (HP) / DAMAGED
~20-40% VALUE
Significant wear — deep creases, heavy edge whitening, major scratches, possible water damage or tears. Generally not investable but still playable in some formats. Very hard to sell at any meaningful price.
PSA GRADING SCALE
PSA (Professional Sports Authenticator) is the most recognized grading company. They grade on a 1-10 scale. A PSA 10 can be worth 3-15x the raw NM price depending on the card.
PSA 10
Gem Mint — Perfect centering (60/40 or better), four perfectly sharp corners, no stains, print defects, or scratches. The rarest grade.
3-15x NM
PSA 9
Mint — One minor imperfection allowed (slight corner wear, minor print defect). Still excellent quality. Much easier to achieve than PSA 10.
1.5-3x NM
PSA 8
NM-Mint — Two minor imperfections. Light edge wear, slight corner rounding. Still a solid grade for most vintage cards.
1.1-1.8x NM
PSA 7
Near Mint — Borders show noticeable fraying, slight corner rounding. Surface may show minor scratching. Often equivalent to raw LP-MP.
~NM price
PSA 1-6
Played to Poor — Significant wear, creases, damage. Generally only worth grading for ultra-rare vintage cards where even damaged copies have value.
Below NM
BGS (BECKETT) GRADING
Beckett uses subgrades (Centering, Corners, Edges, Surface) that average to a final grade. A BGS 9.5 "Gem Mint" is often equivalent to PSA 10 in value.
BGS 10 Black Label
All subgrades 10 — rarest possible, worth 2-5x PSA 10
Rarest
BGS 9.5 Gem Mint
All subgrades 9.5+ — equivalent to PSA 10 in most markets
≈ PSA 10
BGS 9
Mint — similar to PSA 9 but subgrades visible
≈ PSA 9
💡 GRADING TIPS
📦 When to grade: Only grade if PSA 10 pop is low AND the card is worth $50+ raw. Grading costs $15-50+ per card plus shipping.
🔍 Check centering first: Off-center cards cannot achieve PSA 9/10 regardless of condition. Use a ruler — borders should be within 60/40.
💡 Print lines: Some cards have factory print lines that immediately disqualify PSA 10. Check under light before submitting.
🌡️ Storage matters: Store in penny sleeves inside hard cases, away from humidity and light. UV rays fade cards over time.
📊 PSA Pop Report: Check population (how many PSA 10s exist) at psacard.com — low pop = higher premium.
